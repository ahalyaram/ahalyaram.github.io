Why Customer Success?

Bill Gates has launched a new office for COVID vaccine development. AJ Shankar, an entrepreneur in Berkeley, has a startup for litigation support through AI software. Apple has launched A12 Bionic, a 64- bit ARM-based system on a chip (SoC).

Whether you run a hospital, a corporate enterprise, a temple, a wealth management firm, or a university, you have something of value to offer. You seek those that could benefit, establish a relationship, sell or share your offerings, support them through their growth and repeat this symbiosis.

In these journeys, a lot of important decisions revolve around what success means for our customers who buy these products/services, how to measure that, how to achieve that and more importantly, how to scale-up this process as your customer’s needs grow bigger. This is “outcome management”, which is ever more important in these times of shifting sands of COVID lands.

Success matters (matters as in a verb), because they—the Success folks—are the single throat to choke; the cog of continuity before-during-and after a sale; they define success and devise a plan to achieve that success. In addition, Success matters because these folks monitor that plan, marshall the troops when a leak springs in that plan, measure that achievement through business value analyses and helps customers grow and succeed.
